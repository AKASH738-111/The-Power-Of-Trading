Organic vs Chemical Farming

Chemical inputs: kill microbes, reduce soil carbon, cause salt buildup and nutrient lock. Yield rises initially but soil weakens yearly.

Organic inputs: feed microbes, increase humus, improve soil porosity, water holding and natural nutrient cycling. Yield becomes stable and soil fertility increases season by season.

Organic farming converts soil into a living system, while chemical farming turns soil into a feeding medium dependent on fertilizers.
